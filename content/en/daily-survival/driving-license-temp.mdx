If you plan to drive in China during a short visit, your foreign driver's license alone is not sufficient. You need a **Temporary Driving Permit** issued by the local vehicle management office.

## Who Needs a Temporary Driving Permit

Every foreigner who plans to drive in China needs one of the following:
- A Chinese driver's license (requires passing a written exam, even for license holders from most countries)
- A **Temporary Driving Permit** (for short-term visitors)

The temporary permit is the practical option for tourists and short-term business visitors. It is valid for driving the same vehicle categories as your home license.

## Mutual Recognition Agreements

China has driver's license mutual recognition agreements with a limited number of countries. If your country is on this list, the process is simpler:

- **France, Belgium, Serbia, UAE:** Holders of valid licenses from these countries can obtain a Chinese license without taking the written exam. This applies to both full licenses and temporary permits.
- **Most other countries (US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, etc.):** You must apply for the temporary permit through the standard process described below. Your home license is valid as the basis for the application, but not on its own.

If your country is not covered by a mutual recognition agreement, you may still apply for a temporary permit — the process is the same. The difference is whether you can also get a full Chinese license without the exam.

## Required Documents

Gather these before going to the vehicle management office:

1. **Your original foreign driver's license** — must be currently valid
2. **A certified Chinese translation** of your driver's license — this is the most frequently missed item. The translation must be done by a certified translation agency in China. Hotels and friends cannot produce a valid translation. Translation booths are often located near vehicle management offices — expect to pay 100-200 RMB.
3. **Your passport** with a valid entry stamp
4. **Temporary Accommodation Registration slip** — obtained from your hotel or the police station
5. **A medical check certificate** — some cities require this. The medical check is a basic vision test and general health screening, typically available at a designated hospital or clinic near the vehicle management office. Cost is approximately 50-100 RMB.
6. **2-4 passport-sized photos** — white background, recent, similar to passport photo specifications

## Where to Apply

Take all documents to the **Vehicle Management Office** (车辆管理所 / cheliang guanlisuo) in the city where you are staying. Major cities have a dedicated foreign affairs window at their main branch.

**Beijing:** Beijing Vehicle Management Administration, Foreign Affairs Section
**Shanghai:** Shanghai Vehicle Management Office, No. 1790 Hami Road
**Guangzhou:** Guangzhou Vehicle Management Office, Tianhe District

Arrive in the morning — these offices process applications faster earlier in the day, and some stop accepting applications by mid-afternoon.

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Get your license translated.** Find a certified translation service. Search WeChat for translation agencies in your city, or visit a translation booth near the vehicle management office. Confirm they can produce a certified translation specifically for driver's license conversion.

2. **Complete the medical check** if required in your city. Ask the vehicle management office in advance — check their website or call. If required, visit the designated hospital before your appointment.

3. **Go to the vehicle management office.** Bring all seven items listed above. Visit the foreign affairs or international service window.

4. **Submit your application.** The officer reviews your documents, verifies your license type, and enters your information into the system.

5. **Pay the application fee.** The fee is approximately 50-100 RMB, payable in cash or via mobile payment.

6. **Receive your temporary driving permit.** Processing time at the office is typically 30 minutes to 1 hour. You walk out with the permit in hand — no waiting period.

## Validity and Limitations

- **Standard validity:** 3 months from the date of issue
- **Extension:** If your stay exceeds 3 months, you can apply for an extension up to a maximum of 1 year
- **Vehicle category limitation:** You may only drive vehicle categories covered by your home license
- **Geographic limitation:** The permit is valid throughout mainland China

When the permit expires, you must stop driving immediately. There is no grace period.

## Renting a Car

With a temporary driving permit and a valid foreign license, you can rent a car from:
- **International rental chains:** Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have limited presence in major cities
- **Chinese rental platforms:**神州租车 (Shenzhou Car Rental), 一嗨租车 (eHi Car Rental) are the two largest

Car rental companies will want to see both your foreign license and your temporary driving permit. Some require a deposit of 5,000-10,000 RMB, placed as a hold on a credit card (debit cards are often not accepted for deposits). Check that your credit card has sufficient available balance before arriving.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Skipping the translation step.** This is the number one reason applications are rejected. A photo of your license on your phone or a self-translated document is not accepted.
- **Driving with just your foreign license.** This is illegal. In the event of an accident, you are treated as an unlicensed driver — facing fines, possible detention, and full personal liability for all damages.
- **IDP (International Driving Permit).** China has not signed the 1949 Geneva Convention on Road Traffic. An IDP issued in most countries is **not recognized** in China. Do not rely on an IDP alone.
- **Navigation.** Google Maps has limited and often outdated data for China. For driving, use 高德地图 (Amap) or 百度地图 (Baidu Maps) — both are in Chinese but far more accurate for turn-by-turn navigation.

## Alternative Options

If obtaining a temporary permit seems like too much effort:
- **Hire a car with a driver.** Many hotels and travel agencies offer private car hire with a driver for 500-1,000 RMB per day. This is often cheaper and less stressful than renting a car.
- **Use Didi and taxis.** In cities, ride-hailing is widely available, affordable, and eliminates parking and navigation concerns.
- **High-speed rail between cities.** For intercity travel, trains are faster, cheaper, and more comfortable than driving.
