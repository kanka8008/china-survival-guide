The airport departure tax refund has a specific sequence. Missing a step means losing your refund. This guide walks through exactly what to do and when.

## Do You Qualify?

Before you head to the airport, verify you meet all the conditions:

1. **You are a foreign national** departing China after a stay of no more than 183 consecutive days.
2. **You made purchases at stores registered for tax refunds.** Look for a blue "Tax Free" or "退税商店" sign in the store window or at the checkout counter. Not every store participates.
3. **Minimum spend: 500 RMB at a single store on the same day.** You cannot combine receipts from different stores, even in the same shopping mall. The 500 RMB must be on one receipt from one store.
4. **The items are for personal use, not commercial export.**
5. **You depart within 90 days of the purchase date.**
6. **You have the Tax Refund Application Form** from the store. The store fills this out at the time of purchase with your passport number, the item list, and the purchase amount.

### What receipts qualify?

Only receipts from **registered tax refund stores** qualify. The invoice must be a **special VAT invoice** (增值税专用发票), not a generic receipt. The invoice shows:
- The store name and tax registration number
- Your passport name (the store enters this at checkout)
- The item descriptions and prices
- The total amount including VAT

If the receipt does not have your passport name, the refund is rejected at customs. Always confirm the store enters your passport information before you pay.

## Before Leaving for the Airport

1. **Organize your documents:**
   - Tax Refund Application Forms (one per store)
   - Original sales invoices
   - The purchased items themselves
   - Your passport

2. **Pack strategically:**
   - If items are going in **checked luggage**: pack them near the top of your suitcase so they are easy to access. You will need to show them to customs, then re-pack and check the bag.
   - If items are going in **carry-on luggage**: keep them in an easily accessible bag that you can hand to the officer.

3. **Arrive early.** Add 30-45 minutes to your normal airport arrival time. The tax refund process has two separate stops with potential queues at each.

## What the Tax Refund Application Form Looks Like

The form is a multi-part carbon-copy document the store fills out when you buy qualifying items. It contains these sections:

| Section | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Purchaser Information** | Your full name (must match passport), nationality, passport number |
| **Purchase Details** | Store name, invoice number, date of purchase, total amount |
| **Item List** | Each qualifying item listed individually with price |
| **Refund Calculation** | VAT amount, refund rate (11%), estimated refund amount |
| **Refund Method** | You select: cash, bank card, or Alipay at the refund counter |
| **Customs Certification** | A blank area for the customs officer to stamp |

The form is typically triplicate: one copy for the store, one for customs, and one for you (the refund claim copy you present at the airport). Keep all copies together — customs needs to see the full set.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Check In (But Do Not Check Tax Refund Luggage Yet)

Go to your airline's check-in counter. Tell the agent: "I have tax refund items in my checked luggage." The agent will:
- Print your boarding pass
- Tag your luggage normally
- **Return the tagged luggage to you** instead of sending it on the conveyor belt

Do not let them convince you to send the bag through. Once the bag is on the belt, retrieving it for customs inspection is nearly impossible.

### Step 2: Go to the Customs Tax Refund Counter (Before Security)

Find the **Customs Tax Refund** counter in the departure hall, **before** the security checkpoint. Look for signs saying "离境退税" (Departure Tax Refund) or "Customs Tax Refund." The counter is typically near the customs area or the international departures entrance. It is a small desk with green signage and a customs officer seated behind it.

At the counter:
1. Present your **Tax Refund Application Form**, sales invoices, passport, and boarding pass.
2. The officer may ask to see the items. Open your checked bag or carry-on and show the items.
3. The officer compares the items with the form. They verify the items are new, unused, and match the descriptions.
4. The officer stamps your Tax Refund Application Form in the "Customs Certification" section. This stamp is the critical checkpoint — without it, the refund agent after security will not process your refund.
5. If your items were in checked luggage: after the stamp, return to the check-in counter (or the nearby oversized baggage drop) and hand over your suitcase. The agent now sends it through.
6. If your items are in carry-on luggage: keep them with you and proceed to security.

### Step 3: Go Through Security and Immigration

Proceed through security screening and exit immigration as you normally would for any international departure. The stamped refund form stays with you.

### Step 4: Find the Tax Refund Agent (After Security) — The "Green Channel"

After immigration, locate the **Tax Refund Agent** counter in the departure lounge. Look for the green "退税" sign — this is sometimes called the "Green Channel." It is usually near duty-free shops or currency exchange desks. The counter resembles a bank window, with a queue line, a service window, and a cash tray.

At the counter:
1. Present your **customs-stamped Tax Refund Application Form**, passport, and boarding pass.
2. Choose your refund method:

### Refund Methods Compared: Cash vs. Card vs. Alipay

| Method | Processing | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cash (RMB)** | Immediate — the agent counts out RMB banknotes | You walk away with money in hand. No waiting. | You get RMB cash, which you may need to exchange at your destination. |  
| **Bank Card (Visa/Mastercard)** | 7-15 business days (sometimes longer) | Money goes to your card; no cash to carry. | Long wait time. If the transaction fails, resolving it from abroad is difficult. |
| **Alipay** | Within 24 hours | Fastest digital option if you use Alipay. | Only works if your Alipay account is active and verified with your passport. |

**Recommendation:** Choose cash if you want certainty and have time to exchange RMB at your destination. Choose Alipay if you actively use it and want the money in your account quickly. Avoid bank card refund unless you are prepared for a long wait with no easy recourse if there is a problem.

3. If you chose cash, count the banknotes before leaving the counter. The agent provides a receipt with the refund calculation.
4. If you chose bank card or Alipay, keep the confirmation slip. It contains the transaction reference number you need if the refund does not arrive.

### Step 5: Proceed to Your Gate

The tax refund process is complete. Proceed to your gate for boarding.

## Refund Calculation

- **Purchase amount:** The total you paid at the store (including VAT)
- **Base refund rate:** 11% of the purchase amount
- **Agent handling fee:** 2% (deducted by the refund agent)
- **Actual refund you receive:** 9% of the purchase amount

Example: A 2,000 RMB purchase generates a 220 RMB base refund (11%). The agent deducts 40 RMB (2%). You receive 180 RMB (9%).

**The minimum purchase to qualify is 500 RMB at a single store on the same day.** Purchases from different stores cannot be combined, even if they are in the same shopping mall. If you spend 300 RMB at Store A and 300 RMB at Store B in the same mall on the same day, neither qualifies — you need 500 RMB on one receipt from one store.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Going through security first.** The customs stamp counter is before security. This is the most frequent and most costly mistake — once you are through security, you cannot go back, and without the stamp, the refund agent after security will not process your refund.
- **Letting the check-in agent send your checked bag through.** If your tax refund items are in checked luggage, you need the bag with you at the customs counter. Insist on the tagged-but-not-sent procedure. If the check-in agent is unfamiliar with this, ask for a supervisor.
- **Missing the refund agent after security.** Some travelers get the stamp, go through security, get distracted by duty-free shopping, and forget to visit the refund agent. The stamp alone does not trigger a refund — you must present it to the agent.
- **Tax refund items not accessible.** If the items are buried in your carry-on under layers of other items, the officer may not wait for you to unpack everything. Pack refund items in an outer pocket or at the top.
- **Missing items.** The officer expects to see the exact items listed on the Tax Refund Application Form. If you left one item at the hotel or gave it away, the refund for that form may be denied.
- **Counter hours.** Tax refund counters at smaller airports may have limited operating hours — they may not be open for very early morning or late night flights. Check in advance if flying from a secondary airport.
- **Shopping at a non-registered store.** A store displays "Tax Free" but is not actually registered. Verify the store is on the official tax refund store list (ask the store to show their registration certificate) before making a large purchase specifically for the tax refund.

## What If Something Goes Wrong

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Check-in agent sends the bag through before customs | Ask the supervisor to retrieve the bag. Large airports have procedures for this; small airports may not. This is why you must be explicit at check-in |
| Customs counter is closed | This is rare at major airports but possible at secondary airports. If closed and you have no alternative, the refund is lost |
| Lost the Tax Refund Application Form | Cannot be reissued. The refund is lost |
| Forgot items at hotel | Items must be physically present for inspection. Without the items, the refund is denied for that form |
| Agent claims the form is invalid | Ask for the specific reason. Common issues: the store is not registered for tax refunds, the form is incomplete, or the purchase does not qualify (under 500 RMB, excluded category) |
| Refund did not arrive on bank card | Contact the refund agent company with your transaction reference number. This is difficult from abroad — cash avoids this problem entirely |
