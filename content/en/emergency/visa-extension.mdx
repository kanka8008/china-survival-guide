### Can I extend a 30-day visa-free stay?

**Yes, in theory — but approval is not guaranteed.** The Exit-Entry Administration has the authority to extend visa-free stays for "reasonable grounds," but what counts as reasonable is narrowly interpreted.

Extensions are typically granted for:
- **Medical emergencies** — sudden illness or injury requiring continued treatment in China, with hospital documentation
- **Force majeure** — natural disasters, flight cancellations due to extraordinary circumstances, or other events beyond your control that prevent timely departure
- **Unforeseen business necessity** — a business meeting or negotiation that could not reasonably have been scheduled within the original 30 days, with an invitation letter from the Chinese counterpart

Extensions are rarely granted for:
- Wanting to see more places ("I need more time for tourism")
- Convenience ("My friend's wedding is on day 33, not day 28")
- Booking mistakes ("I booked my return flight for day 32 by accident")

### When and Where to Apply

**When:** At least **7 days before** your permitted stay expires. Do not wait until the last day — the office needs time to process your application, and if there are delays, you risk overstaying during the processing period.

**Where:** The **Exit-Entry Administration** (出入境管理局) of the Public Security Bureau in the city where you are staying. Go to the main city-level office, not a district-level police station. Major cities have dedicated counters for foreigner services.

### What the PSB Office Looks Like

The Exit-Entry Administration office is a government service hall. Walking in, you see:
- A large open room with rows of chairs
- Electronic queue number screens above each service counter
- Self-service kiosks near the entrance for photos, copies, and form printing
- Separate zones for Chinese citizens (left side) and foreigners (right side, usually less crowded)
- A reception desk at the entrance where staff check your documents and assign a queue number

Walk to the reception desk. Say: "外国人签证延期" (wàiguórén qiānzhèng yánqī — "Foreigner visa extension"). The staff member hands you a queue ticket with a number and directs you to the waiting area.

### The Application Form — Section by Section

The Exit-Entry Administration provides a printed **Visa/Stay Permit Extension Application Form**. You fill it out by hand (English is acceptable; the form is bilingual in most major cities). Here is what each section asks:

| Section | What You Fill In |
|---|---|
| Personal Information | Full name (as in passport), nationality, date of birth, passport number, passport issue/expiry date |
| Current Visa/Entry | Entry date, port of entry, permitted stay until date, visa category (or "visa-free") |
| Contact Information | Phone number, email, address in China |
| Extension Request | Reason for extension (check a box: medical, business, force majeure, or "other" with written explanation), requested extension period (typically 30 more days) |
| Declaration | Signature and date confirming the information is true |

Submit the completed form at your counter number when called. The officer reviews it, stamps the acceptance copy, and hands you a processing slip.

### Self-Service Photo Booth

Most Exit-Entry Administration offices have a self-service photo booth in the lobby. It looks like a tall narrow cubicle with a curtain. Step inside:

1. Sit on the stool.
2. Follow the on-screen instructions (available in English at major city offices).
3. The camera takes your photo automatically — face forward, neutral expression, white background.
4. The machine prints a strip of 8 passport-sized photos.
5. Cost: 20-30 RMB (insert cash or scan Alipay/WeChat Pay).

Bring your own photos if possible. The booth works, but it can have a queue during peak hours, and the machine only accepts RMB notes or mobile payment.

### The Processing Slip

After you submit your application, the officer gives you an **Application Receipt** (受理回执 / shòulǐ huízhí). This is a half-page printed slip that contains:
- Your name and passport number
- The application reference number
- The date you submitted
- The expected decision date (typically 7 working days later)
- An official stamp

**Keep this slip with your passport at all times.** If a police officer stops you between now and the decision, this slip is your proof that you have a pending extension application and are not overstaying. Without it, you have no evidence.

### What the Approved Extension Looks Like — 30-Day Stamp

If your extension is approved, you receive a **Stay Permit** (停留证件) in your passport. This is either:
- A full-page sticker (similar to a visa sticker) with your photo, the new permitted stay date (entry date + 60 days total), and a hologram, OR
- A rectangular stamp directly on a passport page stating: "准予延期至 [date]" (Extension granted until [date])

The approved duration extends your stay by an additional 30 days from your original deadline. Your total stay becomes 60 days from entry. The stamp or sticker clearly shows the new departure deadline. Check it immediately. If the date is wrong, point it out to the officer before you leave the counter.

### Exact Fee: 160 RMB

The extension application costs **160 RMB**. This is a fixed administrative fee set by the national immigration authority. You pay it at the payment counter inside the Exit-Entry Administration office — a separate window from where you submit your application. Payment is accepted in:
- Cash (RMB)
- UnionPay card
- Alipay / WeChat Pay (scan the QR code at the counter)

The payment receipt is separate from your processing slip. Keep both.

### What is the process?

1. Go to the Exit-Entry Administration office (go in the morning — these offices get crowded).
2. Take a queue number for "外国人签证/居留许可" (Foreigner Visa / Residence Permit).
3. When called, explain your situation and submit your documents.
4. The officer reviews your case and may ask additional questions.
5. If accepted, you receive an **application receipt** — keep this with your passport. This receipt serves as proof that you have a pending extension application if you are questioned before the decision.
6. Wait for the decision. Processing time varies — typically 3-7 working days, but can be longer.
7. If approved, you receive a **stay permit** stamp or sticker in your passport with the new permitted stay date.
8. If denied, you must leave China by your original deadline.

### What documents do I need?

1. **Your passport** (original)
2. **Completed Visa/Stay Extension Application Form** — available at the Exit-Entry Administration office
3. **Recent passport photo** — white background, matching passport specifications
4. **Temporary Accommodation Registration slip** — the form from your hotel or police station
5. **Supporting documents for your extension reason:**
   - Medical: Hospital admission record or doctor's certificate stating you are unfit to travel
   - Business: Invitation letter from the Chinese company, explaining why the extension is necessary. The letter must be on company letterhead with a company seal (公章).
   - Force majeure: Flight cancellation notice, embassy statement, or other official documentation
6. **Copy of your passport information page and entry stamp page** — you can make copies at the office, but bringing your own saves time

### What if my application is pending and my stay expires?

If you submitted the application **before** your stay expired and have the receipt, you are generally considered to have a lawful pending application. In practice, you should stay in the area and be reachable until you receive a decision. This is not a loophole to extend your stay indefinitely — you must still depart immediately if the extension is denied.

### Can I leave and come back for another 30 days?

There is no explicit rule prohibiting same-day exit and re-entry for a new 30-day visa-free stay. However:
- Immigration officers have the discretion to question and refuse entry to anyone they suspect of abusing the policy
- Frequent short-term re-entries (a "visa run" pattern) will be flagged and may result in denied entry
- Each entry is evaluated independently — previous smooth entries do not guarantee the next one

If you genuinely need more time, an extension application is the correct approach. Visa runs are a gamble, and the house has the advantage.

### What happens if I overstay?

See the overstay penalty article for detailed consequences. The short version: fines start at 500 RMB per day and escalate quickly. Serious overstays result in detention and entry bans.
